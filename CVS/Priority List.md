# Microbiology Exam Blueprint: System-Wise Priority & Weightage List

*(Note: This structured syllabus guide is derived entirely from the analysis of previous year questions and standard examination topics to help secure maximum theory marks [1-20].)*

## 1. General Microbiology & Bacteriology

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Sterilization and Disinfection:** You must know the principle, working, controls, and uses of the Autoclave (Moist Heat) and Hot Air Oven (Dry Heat) [21, 22].
*   **Bacterial Genetics:** Focus heavily on methods of gene transfer, specifically Transduction and Conjugation [23-26].
*   **Bacterial Growth:** Draw and label the Bacterial Growth Curve and know the applications of its various stages [27-31].
*   **Koch’s Postulates:** Be prepared to explain these with suitable examples [27, 30, 31].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Culture Media:** Classify with examples, focusing heavily on Enriched, Enrichment, and Transport media, as well as anaerobic media like RCM [25, 30, 32, 33].
*   **Bacterial Capsule:** Know the advantages to microorganisms and the methods of demonstration [31, 34, 35].
*   **Bacterial Genetics:** Understand the differences between mutational and transferable (plasmid-mediated) drug resistance [25, 26, 36].

### Low Yield (★)
*   Microscopes (Dark ground, fluorescent) and differences between Eukaryotes and Prokaryotes [29, 31].
*   Contributions of Louis Pasteur [24, 31].
*   Bacterial flagella and bacterial spores (structure and methods of demonstration) [18, 31, 37].
*   Differences between Exotoxins and Endotoxins [36, 38].

## 2. Immunology

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Hypersensitivity:** Classify reactions based on time of onset and mechanism [39-42]. Prepare detailed mechanisms for Type I (Anaphylaxis mediators), Type III, and Type IV reactions [42-46].
*   **Antigen-Antibody Reactions:** Focus on the principles and applications of Agglutination (Tube agglutination, Widal) and Precipitation reactions, as well as ELISA [26, 38, 47-49].
*   **Autoimmunity:** Memorize the definition, mechanisms, and classification of autoimmune diseases with specific examples [32, 36, 42, 48, 50].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Immunoglobulins:** Detail the structure and functions, particularly of IgG, IgM, and IgA [51].
*   **Immunity:** Detail the differences between active and passive immunity, and the factors involved in innate immunity [43, 51, 52].

### Low Yield (★)
*   Applications of HLA typing and the Anti-globulin (Coombs) test [42, 53, 54].
*   Classical pathway of the Complement system [42].
*   Graft versus host reaction [42, 45].

---

## 3. Bloodstream, CVS & PUO

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Enteric Fever & PUO:** Define Pyrexia of Unknown Origin (PUO) and list its causes [47, 50, 55-58]. Prepare an exhaustive laboratory diagnosis of *Salmonella* Typhi (Enteric Fever), especially differentiating the tests used in the first week versus the Widal test [59-61].
*   **HIV/AIDS:** Draw a well-labeled diagram of the virus [60, 62, 63]. Detail the pathogenesis, opportunistic infections, window period, and NACO testing strategies in India [48, 58, 60, 63-65].
*   **Malaria:** Detail the life cycle and laboratory diagnosis [46, 58, 63, 65, 66]. Specifically focus on the pathogenesis and complications of *Plasmodium falciparum* (Malignant Tertian Malaria) [45, 48, 53, 54, 58, 65].
*   **Syphilis (Spirochetes):** Describe the clinical manifestations (like the primary chancre) and exhaustive serological diagnosis (specific vs. non-specific tests like VDRL) [36, 59, 60, 67-70].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Leishmaniasis:** Detail the *Leishmania donovani* (Kala-Azar) life cycle, pathogenicity, LD bodies, and lab diagnosis [47, 58, 65, 71, 72].
*   **Leptospirosis:** Describe the pathogenesis and laboratory diagnosis, often presented as a clinical case of a sewage worker [60, 73, 74].
*   **Opportunistic Mycoses:** Enumerate the agents and write the lab diagnosis for Candidiasis and Cryptococcosis [58, 61, 63, 65].
*   **Bacterial Endocarditis:** Detail the laboratory diagnosis [24].

### Low Yield (★)
*   Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers (like Dengue pathogenesis) [49, 75].
*   *Wuchereria bancrofti* morphology and life cycle [65, 76].

## 4. Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Vibrio cholerae:** Describe the pathogenesis, halophilic vibrios, and detailed laboratory diagnosis, including transport and enrichment media [18, 32, 55, 70, 77-79].
*   **Intestinal Nematodes:** Describe the life cycle, pathogenicity, and lab diagnosis of *Ascaris lumbricoides* and Hookworm (*Ancylostoma duodenale*) [63, 70, 75, 80].
*   **Shigella & Diarrheagenic E. coli:** Classify and detail the pathogenesis and laboratory diagnosis of bacillary dysentery and various diarrheagenic E. coli types [61, 75, 81].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Cestodes:** Differentiate between *Taenia solium* and *Taenia saginata*, and describe the life cycle and pathogenesis of Neurocysticercosis [75, 79, 82, 83].
*   **Food Poisoning:** Enumerate causative agents and write the pathogenesis/lab diagnosis of Staphylococcal or Clostridial food poisoning [32, 61, 70, 84].
*   **Other Parasites:** *Strongyloides stercoralis* and *Enterobius vermicularis* life cycle and lab diagnosis [34, 35, 41, 70, 79].

### Low Yield (★)
*   Acute Giardiasis findings and *Clostridium difficile* [36, 65, 79].

---

## 5. Hepatobiliary System

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Hepatitis Viruses:** This is highly weighted; focus entirely on Hepatitis B (HBV) morphology, serological markers, pathogenesis, laboratory diagnosis, and post-exposure immunoprophylaxis [39, 40, 70].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Hydatid Cyst:** Detail the *Echinococcus granulosus* life cycle, pathogenesis, and structural diagnosis [48, 70].

---

## 6. Central Nervous System (CNS)

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Pyogenic Meningitis:** Enumerate causative agents and detail the exhaustive laboratory diagnosis, specifically for Meningococcal meningitis [24, 83, 85-87].
*   **Rabies (Rhabdoviruses):** Detail the pathogenesis, laboratory diagnosis, and immunoprophylaxis including non-neural vaccines and the post-exposure schedule [45, 46, 64, 65, 87].
*   **Polio Viruses:** Describe the pathogenesis, pulse polio immunization, and the merits/demerits of Salk vs. Sabin vaccines [49, 69, 83, 87, 88].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Cryptococcal Meningitis:** Detail the fungal CNS infections and laboratory diagnosis [80, 83, 89, 90].
*   **Viral Encephalitis:** Enumerate etiological agents and write the laboratory diagnosis [62, 69].

### Low Yield (★)
*   Prion diseases and *Toxoplasma gondii* (congenital manifestations/calcifications) [45, 91-93].

---

## 7. Respiratory System (RS)

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Pulmonary Tuberculosis:** Detail the *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* morphology, pathogenesis, and exhaustive laboratory diagnosis including recent molecular diagnostic methods [29, 57, 64, 94-96].
*   **Corynebacterium diphtheriae:** Detail the morphology, pathogenesis, and specific toxigenicity tests [18, 29, 30, 64, 95, 96].
*   **Influenza Virus:** Draw a well-labeled diagram, and detail the mechanisms of Antigenic shift and drift along with the laboratory diagnosis [60, 69, 95-97].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Streptococcus spp.:** Differentiate between species, detail the non-suppurative complications of *S. pyogenes*, and outline the *S. pneumoniae* lab diagnosis [24, 29, 30, 68, 95, 98].
*   **Atypical Mycobacteria:** Know Runyon's classification with examples [95, 96].

### Low Yield (★)
*   *Haemophilus influenzae* (Satellitism, X and V factors) [36, 95].
*   Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) and *Mycoplasma pneumoniae* [33, 95, 99].

---

## 8. Musculoskeletal, Skin & Soft Tissue

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Gas Gangrene:** Detail the *Clostridium perfringens* pathogenesis, Nagler reaction principle, and laboratory diagnosis [30, 61, 82, 87, 94, 98].
*   **Dermatophytosis:** Classify the fungi, describe the clinical presentation, and detail the laboratory diagnosis based heavily on microscopic findings [46, 69, 76, 82, 87, 99-101].
*   **Leprosy:** Describe *Mycobacterium leprae* morphology, the differences between Tuberculoid and Lepromatous leprosy, and detail the Lepromin test [55, 64, 99, 101].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Herpes Viruses:** Classify the family, describe *Herpes simplex* lesions/pathogenesis, and outline *Varicella zoster* [80, 86, 87, 94, 101].
*   **Mycetoma:** Enumerate causative agents (Eumycetoma vs. Actinomycetoma differences) and detail the lab diagnosis [64, 93, 101, 102].
*   **Osteomyelitis:** Enumerate causative organisms and outline the lab diagnosis [62, 74].

### Low Yield (★)
*   *Staphylococcus aureus* (MRSA, staphylococcal wound infections) [64, 82, 98, 100].
*   Cutaneous larva migrans and Acanthamoeba keratitis [93, 99].

---

## 9. Genitourinary & Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI):** Enumerate the causative bacteria, define significant bacteriuria, list screening tests, and detail the laboratory diagnosis [32, 69, 70, 91, 92, 103].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Chlamydia trachomatis:** Enumerate the serotypes, diseases caused, and detail the lab diagnosis [70].
*   **Urethritis:** Enumerate the etiological agents and write the lab diagnosis for both Gonococcal and Non-gonococcal urethritis (NGU) [30, 68, 70, 89, 90].

### Low Yield (★)
*   *Trichomonas vaginalis* diagnosis and Chancroid [88, 103].
*   Urinary Schistosomiasis [91, 92].

## 10. Miscellaneous & AETCOM

### High Yield (★★★)
*   **Biomedical Waste Management:** Define the categories, describe the segregation and colour-coding system, and outline disposal methods [29, 47, 50].

### Medium Yield (★★)
*   **Healthcare-Associated Infections (HAI):** Detail the common types and standard/universal safety precautions required to control them [18, 50, 56].
*   **Antimicrobial Stewardship:** Discuss its role in modern microbiology [52].

### Low Yield (★)
*   AETCOM: Reasonable disclosure, patient autonomy versus paternalism, and ethical issues regarding patient sample rejection criteria [28, 52, 54, 58, 74, 104].
