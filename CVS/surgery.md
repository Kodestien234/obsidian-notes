# Surgery Internal Assessment - I (Theory)
**Batch: 2023 | Date: 20/12/2025 | Total Marks: 100**

## Section A - Multiple Choice Questions[cite: 1]

**Q1. A 30-year-old male sustains flame burns to his anterior chest, anterior abdomen, and entire right arm. What is the estimated percentage of total body surface area (TBSA) burned, using the Rule of Nines?**
*   **Answer:** b) 27%[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** The anterior chest accounts for 9%, the anterior abdomen is 9%, and the entire right arm is 9% (9 + 9 + 9 = 27%)[cite: 1].

**Q2. Which method of sterilization is best suited for heat-sensitive surgical instruments?**
*   **Answer:** c) Ethylene Oxide Gas Sterilization[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Autoclaving and boiling rely on high temperatures that destroy heat-sensitive materials, making cold gas sterilization the standard choice[cite: 1].

**Q3. A 45-year-old man is admitted with acute appendicitis. The surgeon decides to perform an open appendectomy. Which historical figure is credited with the first recorded successful appendectomy?**
*   **Answer:** c) Claudius Amyand[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Claudius Amyand performed the first recorded successful appendectomy in 1735[cite: 1].

**Q4. Class III haemorrhagic shock describes blood volume loss as percentage of total?**
*   **Answer:** d) 30-40%[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** The Advanced Trauma Life Support (ATLS) classification defines Class III shock as a 30-40% loss of total blood volume[cite: 1].

**Q5. Most suitable fluid for initial resuscitation in hypovolemic shock is**
*   **Answer:** a) Ringer lactate[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Ringer's Lactate is an isotonic crystalloid fluid universally recommended for initial volume replacement[cite: 1].

**Q6. Phases of wound healing -**
*   **Answer:** a) Inflammatory, proliferation, remodelling[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** These represent the classic three sequential biological phases of wound healing[cite: 1].

**Q7. ARDS results in -**
*   **Answer:** c) Respiratory Alkalosis[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Early Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) causes low blood oxygen, which triggers rapid breathing that excessively blows off carbon dioxide, resulting in alkalosis[cite: 1].

**Q8. All of the following are useful in treatment of Gas Gangrene Except?**
*   **Answer:** d) Anti-Tetanus serum[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Anti-Tetanus serum is specific to tetanus infections, whereas gas gangrene is treated with debridement, antibiotics, and hyperbaric oxygen[cite: 1].

**Q9. Which of the following descriptions of wear edges denote malignancy?**
*   **Answer:** d) Rolled[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Rolled edges are a hallmark clinical sign of Basal Cell Carcinoma, a type of skin cancer[cite: 1].

**Q10. A skin transplant was done from a sister to her brother. After few years, a brother to sister skin transplant was done, but rejection occurred. What is this phenomenon known as?**
*   **Answer:** c) Eichwald Silmser effect[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** This effect describes the rejection of male grafts by female recipients due to an immune response against a male-specific antigen[cite: 1].

**Q11. Ideal steam sterilization of 121°C with pressure of 151 lb/inch2 should have hold time of -** 
*   **Answer:** b) 15 min[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Standard medical autoclaving requires a holding time of 15 minutes to guarantee absolute sterility[cite: 1].

**Q12. Causative organism for Gas Gangrene is -**
*   **Answer:** d) Clostridium[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Gas gangrene is a severe muscle infection primarily caused by the anaerobic bacteria Clostridium perfringens[cite: 1].

**Q13. Cholecystectomy can be classified as**
*   **Answer:** b) Clean contaminated[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Gallbladder removal involves entering the biliary tract under controlled surgical conditions without unusual spillage[cite: 1].

**Q14. In order to provide high-quality CPR, what should the rate of chest compressions per minute be?**
*   **Answer:** c) 100-120[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** American Heart Association guidelines mandate 100 to 120 compressions per minute to maintain adequate blood circulation[cite: 1].

**Q15. Most common site for peripheral aneurysm is -**
*   **Answer:** b) Popliteal[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** The popliteal artery behind the knee is clinically the most frequent site for peripheral arterial aneurysms[cite: 1].

**Q16. Keloid scar is made up of -**
*   **Answer:** a) Dense collagen[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Keloids form from an overactive healing response that deposits thick, disorganized bands of dense collagen[cite: 1].

**Q17. Hyperbaric oxygen is useful in treatment of -**
*   **Answer:** b) Gas gangrene[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** High oxygen tension in the tissues stops the growth and toxin production of the anaerobic Clostridium bacteria[cite: 1].

**Q18. Complication of massive transfusion Include all of the following except -**
*   **Answer:** d) Hypercalcemia[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Massive blood transfusions cause a drop in calcium (hypocalcemia) because the preservatives in stored blood bind to the patient's calcium[cite: 1].

**Q19. What is the cause of Death in Burns within 48 hours?**
*   **Answer:** b) Hypovolemic shock[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** Severe burns cause massive fluid shifts from the blood vessels into the surrounding tissues, leading to fatal drops in blood pressure if not aggressively treated[cite: 1].

**Q20. Which scale is used to evaluate burn depth?**
*   **Answer:** a) Lund and Browder chart[cite: 1]
*   **Rationale:** This chart is the medical standard used to accurately map out burn extent and depth while adjusting for a patient's age and body proportions[cite: 1].

## Section B - Long Answer Questions[cite: 1]

**a) A young boy with H/O trauma 2 day back with crepitus and foul-smelling discharge from wound with hypotension. Explain management of patient pertaining to treatment for gas gangrene.**[cite: 1]
*   **Diagnosis:** Clostridial myonecrosis (Gas gangrene) presenting with septic shock[cite: 1].
*   **Resuscitation:** Immediately secure the airway, provide high-flow oxygen, and administer aggressive IV Ringer's Lactate to correct the low blood pressure[cite: 1].
*   **Medical Therapy:** Start high-dose intravenous Penicillin G combined with Metronidazole or Clindamycin[cite: 1].
*   **Surgical Intervention:** Perform urgent, radical wound debridement to remove all dead muscle until healthy, bleeding tissue is reached[cite: 1].
*   **Advanced Care:** Utilize Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT) to halt the spread of anaerobic bacteria[cite: 1].

**b) A rickshaw puller with non-healing ulcer of 4 X 2 cm over medial aspect of leg. Discuss relevant investigation and treatment.**[cite: 1]
*   **Diagnosis:** Likely a venous ulcer, given the specific location (medial gaiter region) and the patient's occupational history of prolonged standing[cite: 1].
*   **Investigations:** Order a Venous Duplex Ultrasound to evaluate for deep vein thrombosis and venous reflux[cite: 1].
*   **Vascular Testing:** Calculate the Ankle-Brachial Pressure Index (ABPI) to ensure arterial blood flow is sufficient before applying tight bandages[cite: 1].
*   **Conservative Treatment:** Instruct the patient on limb elevation, basic wound care, and apply 4-layer graduated compression bandaging[cite: 1].
*   **Surgical Treatment:** Consider endovenous laser ablation (EVLA) to fix underlying incompetent veins or a skin graft for the ulcer bed once it is clean[cite: 1].

**c) Discuss different methods of sterilization. How will you sterilize instrument after operating HIV positive patient.**[cite: 1]
*   **Physical Methods:** Include dry heat (Hot air oven), moist heat (Autoclaving at 121°C), and gamma radiation for disposable items[cite: 1].
*   **Chemical Methods:** Include Ethylene Oxide (EtO) gas for heat-sensitive items, and cold chemical solutions like 2% Glutaraldehyde[cite: 1].
*   **HIV Protocol (Decontamination):** Immediately submerge all used instruments in 1% Sodium Hypochlorite for 20-30 minutes to rapidly inactivate the virus[cite: 1].
*   **HIV Protocol (Cleaning):** Personnel must wear heavy-duty protective gear and wash the instruments underwater to prevent infectious splashes[cite: 1].
*   **HIV Protocol (Sterilization):** Autoclave the instruments at standard settings, as the HIV virus is highly fragile and easily destroyed by standard heat sterilization[cite: 1].

**d) Describe management of 4-year male child with 18% burn (grade 2).**[cite: 1]
*   **Initial Assessment:** Stop the burning process, secure the airway, and evaluate the precise burn area using a ==pediatric Lund and Browder chart==[cite: 1].
*   **Fluid Calculation:** Use the pediatric formula (3 to 4 ml x body weight in kg x 18% TBSA) to determine the baseline 24-hour Ringer's Lactate requirement[cite: 1].
*   **Fluid Administration:** Administer half of the calculated fluid volume in the first 8 hours, and the remaining half over the subsequent 16 hours[cite: 1].
*   **Maintenance Fluids:** Crucially add dextrose-containing maintenance fluids (like D5 1/2 NS) to prevent life-threatening low blood sugar in pediatric patients[cite: 1].
*   **Local Care:** Provide adequate IV pain relief, clean the wounds gently, and apply topical antimicrobials like silver sulfadiazine[cite: 1].

**e) Describe various blood components and treatment modality for factor X deficiency.**[cite: 1]
*   **Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBCs):** Transfused to treat severe anemia and hemorrhagic shock by rapidly restoring oxygen-carrying capacity[cite: 1].
*   **Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP):** Contains all plasma coagulation factors and is utilized to correct broad, multi-factor bleeding disorders[cite: 1].
*   **Platelets:** Transfused specifically to treat dangerously low platelet counts or acute platelet dysfunction[cite: 1].
*   **Cryoprecipitate:** A highly concentrated source of Fibrinogen, Factor VIII, von Willebrand factor, and Factor XIII[cite: 1].
*   **Factor X Treatment:** Administer Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC) as the primary targeted treatment, or use FFP if PCC is unavailable[cite: 1].

**f) Enumerate prophylactic and therapeutic antibiotics. Describe antibiotics protocols in your institution for post-surgical procedures.**[cite: 1]
*   **Prophylactic Antibiotics:** Administered briefly before surgery (usually within 60 minutes of the incision) to actively prevent surgical site infections[cite: 1].
*   **Therapeutic Antibiotics:** Administered for a prolonged, multi-day course to treat a confirmed, active infection (such as a ruptured appendix)[cite: 1].
*   **Clean Surgeries Protocol:** Standard protocol involves a single dose of IV Cefazolin (1g) given at anesthesia induction for sterile procedures like hernia repairs[cite: 1].
*   **Contaminated Surgeries Protocol:** Standard protocol involves a combination of IV Ceftriaxone and Metronidazole to broadly cover both aerobic and anaerobic bacteria for bowel surgeries[cite: 1].

## Section C - Short Answer Questions[cite: 1]

**a) Universal precaution**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** An infection control system that treats all human blood and bodily fluids as highly infectious materials[cite: 1].
*   **Application:** It mandates the strict use of personal protective equipment (gloves, masks, eyewear) and safe disposal methods for sharps across all patient encounters regardless of known infection status[cite: 1].

**b) Septic shock**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A severe, life-threatening stage of sepsis causing profound drops in blood pressure and cellular dysfunction[cite: 1].
*   **Criteria:** Diagnosed when a patient requires vasopressor medications to maintain a mean arterial pressure above 65 mmHg and exhibits high serum lactate levels despite receiving adequate IV fluids[cite: 1].

**c) HCV (Hepatitis C virus)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A blood-borne RNA virus that targets the liver and causes severe inflammation[cite: 1].
*   **Surgical Risk:** It poses a high occupational hazard for surgeons via accidental needle-stick injuries and can progress to chronic cirrhosis or liver cancer in infected patients[cite: 1].

**d) Marjolein's ulcer**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** An aggressive, malignant skin cancer (most commonly Squamous Cell Carcinoma)[cite: 1].
*   **Etiology:** It uniquely develops in areas of chronic skin inflammation, such as long-standing unhealed burn scars, radiation scars, or chronic venous ulcers[cite: 1].

**e) TPN (Total Parenteral Nutrition)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** The continuous intravenous administration of complete daily nutritional requirements (carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins) directly into the bloodstream via a central vein[cite: 1].
*   **Indication:** Used strictly when the patient's gastrointestinal tract is entirely non-functional, physically inaccessible, or requires complete bowel rest for healing[cite: 1].

**f) SSTI (Skin and Soft Tissue Infection)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A broad medical category encompassing bacterial infections that affect the skin layers and underlying soft tissues[cite: 1].
*   **Spectrum:** Conditions range from mild, easily treatable cellulitis to severe, rapidly spreading surgical emergencies like necrotizing fasciitis[cite: 1].

**g) Green corridor organ transplant**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A specialized traffic management protocol organized by local law enforcement to transport harvested human organs[cite: 1].
*   **Purpose:** It guarantees a dedicated, unhindered route for the transport ambulance to minimize transit time and keep the donor organ perfectly viable for the recipient[cite: 1].

**h) VAP (Ventilator associated Pneumonia)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A specific type of hospital-acquired lung infection that frequently develops in critically ill ICU patients[cite: 1].
*   **Timing:** Diagnosed specifically when the pneumonia occurs more than 48 hours after a patient has been intubated and placed on a mechanical breathing machine[cite: 1].

**i) Myoglobin urea (Myoglobinuria)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** The abnormal presence of myoglobin (a specialized muscle protein) in the urine, giving it a distinct dark, tea-like appearance[cite: 1].
*   **Clinical Danger:** It serves as a major warning sign of severe muscle breakdown (rhabdomyolysis) and poses a massive risk of causing acute kidney failure[cite: 1].

**j) GVHD (Graft versus Host Disease)**[cite: 1]
*   **Definition:** A severe, often fatal immune complication that occurs following allogeneic tissue or stem cell transplants[cite: 1].
*   **Mechanism:** The newly transplanted donor immune cells recognize the recipient's entire body as foreign and systematically attack the recipient's skin, liver, and gastrointestinal tract[cite: 1].
